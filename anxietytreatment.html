How is Anxiety diagnosed?

Anxiety is diagnosed through a detailed questionaire that asks about symptoms. The more detailed the answers are, the better.

Usually, if the symptoms affect every day life such as school, work, or social life, it hightens the change of being diagnosed. The questionaire may also pick up on other mental illnesses such as depression.

What are the treatment options?


Cognative Behavioural Therapy

Cognative Behavioural Therapy, also known as CBT is a form of therapy that works towards restructuring negative thought patterns and change how different situations are approached.

Exposure Therapy

Exposure Therapy is a technique that helps people overcome fears and anxieties by breaking the pattern of avoidance and fear. Exposure Therapy is known to begin working anywhere between a few weeks to a few months.

Complemental health techniques

Self-management stratagies such as stress management can help treat Anxiety. Examples of this technique includes: Medetation, Yoga, Mindfulness, Acupuncture, and other stress-relief strategies.

Medication

If it's thought that medication would best suit someone's situation, medication such as antidepressants and antianxiety drugs can be prescribed by a doctor.

Natural Remedies for Anxiety

Sometimes, lifestyle changes can be enough to relieve life stresses and Anxiety symptoms. These strategies can help cope with everyday life by implementing healthy activities into someone's lifestyle.

These 'Natural Remedies' can include:
• Healthy sleep patterns
• Exercise
• Healthy dieting choices
• Alcohol avoidance
• Drug avoidance
• Caffeine avoidance
• Quiting smoking
