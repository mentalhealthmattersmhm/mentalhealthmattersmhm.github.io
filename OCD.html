What is OCD?

OCD, short of Obsessive Compulsive Disorder is a mental disorder that involves patterns of unwanted thoughts, fears, obsessions, and repetitive behaviours that interfer with daily life.

5 Common myths about OCD


OCD is just cleanliness

Although cleanliness is a common compulsion of OCD, it is not the only one. Common compulsions of OCD can include things such as:
• Hoarding
• Checking things
• Repetative behaviours (writing, reading, tapping)
• Counting
• Praying

Only women have OCD

Experts have found that Obsessive Compulsive Disorder affects people regardless of sex, background, ethnicity, and race. The reason it is commonly thought that only women have OCD is because men are more likely to be diagnosed in children however in adolescence and adulthood, women are more likely to be diagnosed. About 1.5 to 1 which makes it almost equal.

OCD isn't treatable

OCD has been found to not have a cure, though it is treatable. Symptoms, such as compulsions can be managed with specific treatments that can vary from therapies such as exposure therapy to medication.

We’re all ‘a little OCD’

A common misconseption is that OCD is just a personality trait, however this idea surrounding OCD is completely wrong. Obsessive Compulsive Disorder is a serious mental disorder that involves high levels of Anxiety and emotional distress.

People with OCD just need to relax

Most people believe that if you have OCD, relaxing should fix the problem, however for many diagnosed with this condition, it's not possible. Some think that OCD is just an overreaction to life stresses and they just need to relax, however in reality, OCD is uncontrollable fears and compulsions that can interfer with a persons daily life.

Causes

The specific cause for OCD has yet to be found and understood, however there have been some established risk factors and possible causes:
• Changes in brain functions
• OCD may be caused by genetics
• The condition can be learned from watching someone else

Some risk factors can include:
• Family history
• Trauma and/or stress
• Personality
• Abuse
• Other mental health disorders

Symptoms of OCD


Obsessive Symptoms

• Worries about germs, dirt, or illness
• Fears of saying something offensive or obscene
• A need to have your possessions aligned, orderly, or symmetrical
• Explicit sexual or violent thoughts
• Worries about throwing things away
• Questioning your sexual desires or orientation
• Worries about the health and safety of yourself or your loved ones
• Intrusive images, words, or sounds

Compulsive Symptoms

• Washing your hands, objects, or body
• Organizing or aligning objects in a specific way
• Counting or repeating specific phrases
• Touching something a set number of times
• Seeking reassurance from others
• Collecting certain objects or buying several of the same item
• Hiding objects you could use to hurt yourself or someone else
• Mentally going over your actions to make sure you haven’t harmed anyone else

OCD vs OCPD

Although similar, Obsessive Compulsive Disorder and Obsessive Compulsive Personality Disorder are two different mental disorders with different clinical definitions.

Symptoms

Some symptoms of the two do overlap, such as the similar thoughts and behaviours, however there are some differences.

OCD is a mental disorder characterised by by intrusive thoughts and compulsive behaviours. Symptoms of OCD can fluctuate with Anxiety and individuals with OCD are usually aware.

OCPD on the other hand is a personality disorder where the individual experiences constant feelings of wanting to be in control. Symptoms of this can include behaviours such as stubborness, perfectionism, and attention to detail.
